2. Probability Theory (確率) ¶

2.1. Definition ¶

$$ \mathbb{P} \approx \frac{n_A}{n}\\[10pt] $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{P}[A] &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{n_A}{n} \\ \mathbb{P}[A] &= \frac{N_A}{N} \end{aligned} $$

$n$ : a number of total observation（観察数）
$n_A$ a number of event A occured（特定のイベントが起きた数）

2.2. Axiomatic Definition（確率公理）¶

  1. $\mathbb{P}[A] \geq 0$
  2. $\mathbb{P}[\Omega]=1$
  3. $\mathbb{P}[A \cup B]=\mathbb{P}[A] + \mathbb{P}[B]$ if only if events A and B are mutually exclusive, and A and B are independent
  4. $\mathbb{P}[A \cap B] = \mathbb{P}[A] \times \mathbb{P}[B]$ if only if events A and B both occur, and A and B are independent
  5. $\mathbb{P}[A \cap B] = \mathbb{P}[B] \times \mathbb{P}[A|B]$ if only if events A and B both occur, and A and B are dependent

Quiz:
１）二つの賽ごろを投げるとき、３或いは６になる確率
２）二つの賽ごろを投げるとき、３と６になる確率

Answer:
１）二つの賽ごろを投げるとき、３或いは６になる確率
$\frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}$

２）二つの賽ごろを投げるとき、３と６になる確率
$\frac{1}{6} \times \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{36}$

2.3. Conditional Probability（条件付き確率）¶

$$ \mathbb{P}[B|A] = \frac{ \mathbb{P}[A \cap B]}{ \mathbb{P}[B]} $$ Often one event will affect (be depend on) the outcome of another.
The probability that $B$ takes a particular value given that $A$ has taken a particular value.
「A が起こったときの B の（条件付き）確率」「条件 B の下での A の確率」

2.4. Bayes' Theorem（ベイズの定理）¶

$$ \mathbb{P}[B|A] = \frac{ \mathbb{P}[A|B] \mathbb{P}[B] }{ \mathbb{P}[A] } $$

Bayes' theorem converts test result into the real probability of the event, by updating probabilities with prior knowledge.

  • $P[B|A]$: posterior probability. 事象 A が起きた後での、事象 B の確率 (事後確率)
  • $P[B]$: prior probability. 事象 A が起きる前の、事象 B の確率（事前確率)
  • $P[A|B]$: likelihood. (尤度)
  • $P[A]$: normalizing probability. (標準化要素)

e.g.1 癌になる確率は1%
癌があって健診で癌が分かる確率は80%
健診を受けて癌が分かる確率は？

cancer 1% no cancer 99%
test positive 80% 9.6%
test negative 20% 90.4%

$$ \begin{aligned} A &= \text{テストでポジティブになる確率} \\ B &= \text{癌がある確率} \end{aligned} $$

$$ \mathbb{P}[B \mid A] = \frac{ \mathbb{P}[A \mid B] \mathbb{P}[B]}{ \mathbb{P}[A] } $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{P}[A \mid B] &= 0.8\\ \mathbb{P}[B] &= 0.01\\ \mathbb{P}[A] &= 0.8 \times 0.01 + 0.096 \times 0.99 = 0.10304\\[10pt] \therefore \mathbb{P}[B \mid A] &= \frac{0.8 \times 0.01}{0.10304} = 0.0776 \approx 7.8\% \end{aligned} $$

4. Distribution Function (分布関数）¶

Probability distribution comprises of the set of posible outcomes (all of the $x$) and their probabilities $\mathbb{P}[X]$ in order to explain how the total probability 1 is distributed (or shared out) over all possible outcomes.
分布関数はトータル確率１がどのように分布されていることを説明するために、全ての可能なｘセットとその確率$\mathbb{P}[X]$で構成される。

$$ \begin{aligned} F_X &= \mathbb{P}[X \leq x], \\[5pt] &\text{ where } x \in (-\infty, \infty) \end{aligned} $$

  • $F_X(-\infty )= 0 $ and $F_X(\infty )= 1 $
  • If $F_X(x_0) = 0$ then $F_X(x) = 0$ for all $x \leq x_0$
  • $ \mathbb{P}[X > x] = 1 - F_X(x)$
  • $\mathbb{P}[x_1 < X \leq x_2] = F_X(x_2) - F_X(x_1)$

6. Joint Probability (同時分布) ¶

Fig reference: By IkamusumeFan - Own work, CC BY-SA 3.0

$$ \begin{align} \mathbb{P}(Y=y_1, Y=y_2, Y=y_3, \cdots, Y=y_j) &= \mathbb{P}(y_1, y_2, y_3, \cdots, y_j) \\ & = \mathbb{P}[y_1] \times \mathbb{P}[y_2] \times \mathbb{P}[y_3] \times \cdots \mathbb{P}[y_j]\\ &= \prod_{j=1}^{j} \mathbb{P}[y_j], \text{ if only if all } y_j \text{ is independent} \end{align} $$

8. Marginalisation Probability (周辺分布) ¶

If the result would only expresses a specific outcome, it can be obtained by marginalising out independent random variables from the joint distribution.
同時分布から独立な確率変数を排除することで、イベントがただ一つの特定な結果を持つことを計算できる。

Fig reference: By IkamusumeFan - Own work, CC BY-SA 3.0

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{p}[Y=y] &= \sum_x \mathbb{p}[Y=y, X=x] \\ & = \sum_x \mathbb{p}[Y = y \mid X = x] \mathbb{P}[X = x] \end{aligned} $$

9. Bayes' Theorem ¶

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{P}[X=x \mid Y=y] &= \frac{ \mathbb{P}[Y=y \mid X=x] \mathbb{P}[X=x] }{ \mathbb{P}[Y=y] } \\ &= \frac{ \mathbb{P}[Y=y \mid X=x] \mathbb{P}[X=x] }{ \sum_n \mathbb{P}[Y=y|X=x_n] \mathbb{P}[X=x_n] } \end{aligned} $$

With random variables and p.d.f., $f_X(x)$

$$ f_{X \mid A} = \frac{ \mathbb{P}[A \mid X = x] }{ \mathbb{P}[A] } f_X(x) = \frac{ \mathbb{P}[A \mid X = x] f_X(x) }{ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \mathbb{P}[A \mid X=x] f_X(x)dx } $$

10. Expectations (期待値) ¶

Expectation tells what value we would expect for some function $f_X(x)$ of a random variable $X$ to take and is defined.
期待値または平均は、確率変数の実現値を, 確率の重みで平均した値である。

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{For continuous random variable:} & \\ &\mathbb{E}_{F(x)}[f_X(x)] = \mu_x = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f_X(x)dx \end{aligned} $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{For discrete random variable:} & \\ &\mathbb{E}_{P(x)}[f_X(x)] = \mu_x = \sum_i P_X(x_i) x_i \end{aligned} $$

10.1 Mean (平均値) ¶

The mean is an example of an expectation.
平均値は期待値の一つである。

Example :
Expected value of $X, f(x)=X$ in one die.

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{p(x)}[X] &= \sum_x \mathbb{P}[X] \\ &= \sum_x x \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{2}{6} + \cdots + \frac{6}{6} = \frac{21}{6} = 3.5 \end{aligned} $$

$\clubsuit$ Three case of expectation

  • $\text{if } f(x) = aX$ : $$\mathbb{E}[f(x)] = f(\mathbb{E}[X])$$

  • $\text{if } f(x) = a$ : $$\mathbb{E}[f(x)] = a$$

  • $\text{if } \mathbb{E}[f(x) + g(x)]$ : $$\mathbb{E}[f(x)] + \mathbb{E}[g(x)]$$

10.2 Variance (分散) ¶

Variance is a measure of how variable the random variable is.
確率変数の2次の中心化モーメント. これは確率変数の分布が期待値からどれだけ散らばっているかを示す非負の値である.

$$ \sigma^2_x = \mathbb{E}\big[ (X - \mu_X)^2 \big] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x - \mu_X)^2 f_X(x) dx $$

$\clubsuit$ Moment : $$ \mathbb{E}\big[ (X)^p \big] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x^p f_X(x) dx $$

$\clubsuit$ Central Moment : $$ \mathbb{E}\big[ (X - \mu_X)^p \big] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x - \mu_X)^p f_X(x) dx $$

11. Covariance (共分散) ¶

  • A measure of the joint variability of two random variables.
  • The sign of the covariance therefore shows the tendency in the linear relationship between the variables.
  • 変数の間の可変性を表す
  • 符号により線型的な可変性が分かる

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Cov}[X, Y] &= \Sigma[X] = \mathbb{E}[ ( \mathbf{X} - \mathbb{E}[ \mathbf{X} ] ) ( \mathbf{Y} - \mathbb{E}[ \mathbf{Y} ] ) \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{ (x_i - \bar{x}) (y_i - \bar{y}) }{N} \end{aligned} $$

Fig renference: Texts in Computer Science

11.1. Covariance Matrix (共分散行列) ¶

When dealing with vectors, variance is generalised to a covariance matrix.
2組の対応するデータ間での、平均からの偏差の積の平均値である。

$$ \text{Cov}[X] = \Sigma[X] = \mathbb{E}[ ( \mathbf{X} - \mathbb{E}[ \mathbf{X} ] ) ( \mathbf{X} - \mathbb{E}[ \mathbf{X} ]^\top ) $$

Structure of covariance matrix $$ \begin{bmatrix} \text{var}_{11} & \text{cov}_{12} & \dots & \text{cov}_{1n} \\ \text{cov}_{21} & \text{var} & \dots & \text{cov}_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \text{cov}_{n1} & \text{cov}_{n2} & \text{cov}_{n3} & \text{var} \end{bmatrix} $$

11.2. Pearson Correlation Coeficient (相関係数) ¶

相関係数は -1 から 1 までの値をとる。1 であれば 2 つの変数の値は完全に同期していることになる。

$$ \begin{aligned} \rho_{X, Y} = \frac{ \text{Cov}[X, Y] }{\sigma_X \sigma_Y} ,\quad \text{where} &\\ &\text{Cov is the covariance}\\ &\sigma_{X} \text{is the standard deviation of }X\\ &\sigma_Y \text{is the standard deviation of } Y \end{aligned} $$

12. Gaussian Distribuition (正規分布、またはガウス分布) ¶

$$ P(y \mid \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{- \frac{1}{2 \sigma ^2} (y - \mu)^2 } $$

平均値の付近に集積するようなデータの分布を表した連続的な変数に関する確率分布である。

12.1 Multivariate Gaussian (多変数正規分布) ¶

For $\mathbf{X} = {x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_D}$, $$ P(\mathbf{X}) = \frac{1}{2 \pi^{ D/2 } } \mid\Sigma \mid ^{1/2} exp \big[ - \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{X} - \mathbf{\mu})^\top \Sigma^{-1} (\mathbf{X} - \mathbf{\mu}) \big] $$

1.3. Choose Algorithm: Gaussian Naive Bayes Classifier¶

Recipe 1) Bayes' theorem

$$ \text{posterior} = \frac{ \text{prior} \times \text{likelihood} }{ \text{evidence} } $$

Explain on the white board!

Recipe 2) Guasian Distribution to find conditional probability
$$p(x_i \mid y) = \frac{1}{ \sqrt{2 \pi \sigma_y^2} } \text{exp} \big( - \frac{ (x_i - \mu_y)^2 }{ 2 \sigma_y^2 } \big)$$

Recipe 3) Navie conditional independence assumption
Assumtion that each feature $x_{i}$ is conditionally independent of every other feature $x_{j}$ for $j \neq i$, given the category $C_{k}$.

$$ p(x_1 \mid x_2, x_3, \cdots, x_n, C_k) = p(x_1|C_k) $$

Reference: Wiki

$$ \begin{aligned} p(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4, C_k) &= p(x_1 \mid x_2, x_3, x_4, C_k)p(x_2, x_3, x_4, C_k)\\ &\vdots \\ &= p(x_1 \mid x_2, x_3, x_4, C_k)p(x_2 \mid x_3, x_4, C_k)p(x_3, x_4, C_k) \cdots p(x_4 \mid C_k)p(C_k) \\[10pt] &\downarrow \text{ using navie condition independence assuption} \\[10pt] &=p(x_1 \mid C_k)p(x_2 \mid C_k)p(x_3 \mid C_k)p(x_4 \mid C_k)p(C_k) \\ &=p(C_k) \prod_{i=1}^{4} p(x_i \mid C_k) \end{aligned} $$
